Hi! I'm a first-year Master's student in the Machine Learning Department at [Carnegie Mellon University](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/). Previously, I studied computer science and mathematics at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, where I rowed for the men's varsity crew team. 

Research interests
======
I'm broadly interested in developing safe and robust [foundation models](https://www.adalovelaceinstitute.org/resource/foundation-models-explainer/), particularly with applications to healthcare. This semester, I'm working with [Virginia Smith](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~smithv/) on machine unlearning and cross-silo federated learning systems.

In addition to my primary focus in Machine Learning, I also work with Harvard Medical School and Brigham and Women's Hospital, where I am involved in discovering biomarkers and personalized medicine for cancer.

Past Projects
======

This past summer, I worked at [GoDaddy Inc](https://www.godaddy.com/airo) as a Machine Learning Scientist Intern. I ran experiments with Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) to improve their support article retrieval pipeline and developed proof-of-concept for a first-of-kind retrieval-augmented task automation AI agent.

For my senior thesis, I developed a [new deep learning framework](https://neilkale.github.io/portfolio/portfolio-2/) with Professor Randy Paffenroth at WPI. Our work aims to build more interpretable and finetunable neural networks.

During my undergrad, I conducted research at Dr. David Kwiatkowski's cancer genetics lab for 3 years. My work involved analyzing RNA sequencing data to investigate biomarkers for neoadjuvant immunotherapy response.

Our work from 2021-2023 is published in the high-impact [Cancer Cell](https://www.cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108%2823%2900321-5) journal. A similar project which I spearheaded in Summer 2023 is documented in [my portfolio](https://neilkale.github.io/portfolio/portfolio-1/).
